# CASE-006: Offshore Vendor Continuity Under Crisis (Qu POS → Curve Dental)

**Date of decision:** [JET to confirm — approximate window only, Qu POS tenure]
**Tier:** CASE

## Context
At Qu POS, JET relied on an offshore provider for customer support
coverage. A typhoon destroyed that provider's operating capacity
overnight, with no warning window — support coverage for Qu POS
customers was at immediate risk with no existing contingency vendor
under contract.

## Decision
JET sourced, vetted, and contracted a replacement offshore provider
(Connext Global) from scratch, writing the SLAs and contract terms
personally, and onboarded the new provider fast enough to avoid
significant service disruption.

## Why this approach (not just what)
One judgment call stands out: JET treated the replacement as a vendor
relationship to be built properly — writing contract terms and SLAs
from scratch rather than taking the fastest available stopgap — even
under acute time pressure. That decision paid forward: the same
provider relationship was later brought to Curve Dental, where it was
used again (per the Repository Growth Rule, this is the second
